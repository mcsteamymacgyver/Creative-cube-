About This Project

Why we built Lost & Found, what problem we saw in our Village, and how this website is trying to fix it.

The Problem We Noticed

A real situation many people face every day.

What We Observed

As students, we noticed something that happens all around us people lose things every single day in our Village.

And the sad part? Most of those items are never returned. Not because nobody found them but because there was no way for the finder to reach the owner.

This is the gap we decided to fill.

  • No single place to report a lost item
  • Honest finders don't know who to call
  • Items sit in offices for months unclaimed
  • Important documents like IDs get lost for good
  • Low-income people suffer the most from these losses

Our Solution

We created Lost & Found a simple website where anyone can report a lost item or a found item. The site helps connect owners with finders in a safe verified way.

Think of it like a community notice board but online, available 24/7, and smarter. When a match is found, we reach out to the owner and verify their identity before the item is returned.

No registration. No fees. No complicated steps. Just a simple form and a community that cares.

Where Items Are Commonly Lost

  • Taxis and bus parks
  • Markets and shopping areas
  • Schools, colleges & universities
  • Hospitals & health centres
  • Churches & mosques

How We Make Sure the Right Person Gets the Item

This was the most important part of our design making sure nobody falsely claims an item that isn't theirs.

You Give Specific Details

When you report your item, we ask you to describe it in detail colour, brand, what's inside, any scratches, unique features. Details only you would know.

We Ask Verification Questions

When your item is found, we contact you and ask questions based on what you told us. If your answers match, we know it's you.

Safe Handover

Once we're sure, we connect you with the finder and arrange a safe, convenient way for you to collect your item.

This system means that even if someone tries to fake being the owner, they won't be able to pass the verification because they weren't there when the item was reported.
